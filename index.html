Andrei González Galeano

PhD student in Mathematics at CIMAT

Short Bio

Hi! I'm Andrei, a PhD student in Mathematics at CIMAT (Centro de Investigación en Matemáticas) in Mexico, under the supervision of Professor Ignacio Barradas and Professor Geiser Villavicencio.

I am an applied mathematician specializing in the mathematical modeling of infectious diseases. More generally, my research interests lie in the application of mathematical modeling and simulation methods to address problems in biology, ecology, and public health.

Before joining CIMAT, I completed my Master's degree in Mathematics at Universidad EAFIT in Colombia under the supervision of Professor Jairo Villegas, where I developed a strong interest in mathematical modeling.

You can check my CV.

Research

My main area of work is the mathematical modeling and analysis of epidemiological models. I focus on understanding the dynamics of infectious diseases through compartmental models and the development of mathematical tools to analyze and control such systems.

Currently, I am working on:

  • Bifurcation analysis in compartmental epidemiological models
  • Development of optimal control strategies in epidemiological models
  • Qualitative analysis techniques in ordinary differential equations
  • Threshold parameter analysis in mathematical epidemiology

I am always open to discussions and collaborations in these areas — feel free to reach out!
